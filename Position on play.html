Position on play:


  • Malaguzzi's theory comprises three essential principles that form the basis of the Reggio Emilia Approach, one of which is play, and is a crucial component of children's learning and development.


  • The Reggio Emilia approach emphasises the use of play as a learning tool. Children's interest, inquiries, and the world in which they live guide play. The play resources supplied contribute meaningfully to the children's play. Children's ability to design their own plays and discoveries assists in the development of autonomy and creativeness (Ebbeck & Waniganayake, 2017).


  • As children direct their own play, social interactions occur and relationships are developed. Language is thrived, science and maths are examined, creativeness and arts go hand - in - hand, nature is incorporated across many play settings and discoveries, and the cultures of others is meaningfully experienced (Ebbeck & Waniganayake, 2017).


  • Children are fully immersed in their play. Educators use provocations to inspire debate, investigation, experimentation, curiosity, and understanding after observing the children. Provocations are focused on topics that the children are interested in (McCormick Smith & Cho, 2018).


  • Provocation can be a concept i.e. change, a problem i.e. children touching chrysalises and engaging them, an object i.e. a map, questions from the children and teacher, an event, a book and nature e.g. children’s fascination with trees (Edwards & Gandini, 2018).


  • Environments are meant to foster inclusivity and social connections, engagement with a variety of resources, as well as accessibility to the outdoor and the local communities (Lino & Parente, 2018).
