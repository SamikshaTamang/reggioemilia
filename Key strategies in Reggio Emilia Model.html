Key strategies for:

Teaching

Listen to the children, observe their engagement, and document their learning (Bucher, 2020). Teacher will collaboratively be engaged with the children (Wolfe et al., 2021).

learning

Reggio Emilia stresses on the term hundred languages of children that illustrate that children have different avenues for thinking, constructing, negotiating, developing, revising, and expressing their thoughts, emotions, and feelings (Kaynak et al., 2021).

assessment

The core principle of Reggio Emilia regarding assessment is to make children learning and thinking visual. For this, teachers are able to choose variety of assessment techniques (Heineman, 2022).

Planning

Teacher will facilitate children learning through planning activity base on the interest of the children (Fochi, 2022).

Reflection

Teachers take on some time to stop, challenge, change, and think on their ongoing teaching practices or environment for the children (Sorzio & Campbell-Barr, 2019).

Goal and outcomes:

The aim of this approach is to teach children how they would be able to use the symbolic languages for instance, sculpting, drama, painting and more (Aljabreen, 2020).
