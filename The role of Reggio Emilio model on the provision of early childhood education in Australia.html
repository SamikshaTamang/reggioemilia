The influence of Reggio Emilio model on the provision of early childhood education in Australia:

In 1995, Millikan was formally introduced as the Reggio Emilia Liaison for New Zealand and Australia by Reggio Children following her trips to the United States and to Reggio Emilia in 1990 (Hall, 2013). With her advocacy efforts, many Australian educators and international attendees on her conferences and seminars were influenced and the model was started to be accepted in the educational system of Australia.

  • More modern concepts about children's development and learning are based on Reggio Emilia's philosophical leanings as well as sociocultural theory (Hall, 2013). The Australian Early Years Learning Framework, which emphasises this theoretical approach, reflects this notion of transformation (Department of Education, Employment, Workplace and Relation, 2009a).
  • Furthermore, current theory and study influenced by the Reggio Emilia method appreciate and acknowledge the environment as a third teacher. It includes physical components if the space such as types of furniture, resources, lighting, and architecture (Early Childhood Australia, n.d.).
  • Inclusive environments acknowledge the individuality of each child considering the age, interests, preferences, strengths, abilities, and learning styles of a child and supports their learning and development by providing appropriate learning experiences (Australian Children’s Education & Care Quality Authority [ACECQA], 2018).
  • The quality Area 3 of the National Quality Standard emphasises the need of creating sustainable and inclusive learning settings which enhance and foster children's learning (NQS) especially area 3.2: The early childhood service environment is welcoming, supportive, and encourages discovery and play-based learning.
  •  Likewise, Reggio Emilia approach highly emphasis on the relationships between children, teachers, families, and communities and believes relationships are the foundations of learning (Follari, 2015). This believe is practiced in daily curriculum of Australian early childhood education as relationships between the stakeholders are very important for supporting the holistic development and learning of children (ACECQA, 2018).
  • In Reggio Emilio model teachers and families are the most essential partners in children’s learning. This reflects to the current Australian early years learning framework which states that responsive learning relationships are strengthened as educators and children learn together and share decisions, respect and trust (DWEER, 2009, p.15).
