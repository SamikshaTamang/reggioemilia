The role of the program model on the provision of early childhood education across the globe:

How could a little town in Northern Italy have shaped educational perspectives in several countries? Since its inception in the 1940s, this early childhood education programme concept has been used by early childhood educators all over the world. Numerous preschools are using a child-led curriculum based on the Reggio Emilia Approach. It is now usual practise to tailor educator's curriculum to the individual interests of the children in classroom. For example, if numerous children develop an interest in sea creatures, opportunities to strengthen maths, critical thinking, and language abilities can be built around this specific subject.

Unites states
The National Center for Children in Poverty (NCCP) (n.d.) discovered some intriguing results that highlight the need of delivering a curriculum based on early children's social-emotional development. By adopting programmes for young children which promote socio-emotional skills, the probability of these children acquiring mental illnesses as adults is significantly lowered. Preschools were established which supported both emotional and cognitive development (Sattar, 2019).



 The influence of Reggio Emilio model on the provision of early childhood education in South America Red SOLARE is the Latin-American association that supports and disseminates this philosophy in Argentina, Paraguay, Brazil, Colombia, Mexico, Peru, Costa Rica, Chile, Ecuador, Guatemala, Dominican Republic, Uruguay and Venezuela.

Brazil
Red SOLARE provides teachers and schools with opportunities to participate in conferences and seminars to learn more about this philosophy and how to implement its values into their own schools (Red SOLARE Brazil, 2015).


Colombia
Redd SOLARE creates a network among its members to develop communication, cooperation and exchange of experience, research, and theories, and to always generate many new dialogues on the role of children in education, culture, and society (Red SOLARE Colombia, 2015).
 The influence of Reggio Emilio model on the provision of early childhood education in Europe


Sweden
The Reggio Emilia School in Sweden provides a variety of training options. Its programme is available to everyone. The programme is broken into two parts: Around the Country and Stockholm (Foerch & Iuspa, 2016). Furthermore, it offers possibilities for professional advancement at the early childhood setting and school premises, demanding the training thru the programme Corporate Education.


South Africa
Leading to variations in its educational system, Africa has shown resurgence of interest in using the Reggio Emilia approach. An exemplar of such an implementation can be seen at South Africa's St. Mary's School. Using engaged listening, they provide opportunities for exploration and participate with children in their joy as well as interest in constructing, investigating, and learning (Foerch & Iuspa, 2016).
