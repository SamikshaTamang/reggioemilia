Period of Conception:

1960-1963

Within the town of Villa Gaida, the district of Reggio Emilia made a metropolitan preschool in 1913, a typical school that provided physical and ethical instruction for children and preschool instruction (Cagliari et al., 2016).

1963

The Robinson Preschool was the first preschool for children aged 3-6 years old, founded by the municipality of Reggio Emilia.

1971

Loris Malaguzzi established centres for children aged 0-2 years (Thornton & Brunton, 2014).

1981

The Hundred Languages of Children exhibition in Reggio Emilia included a collection of educational projects created by municipal infant-toddler centres and preschools (Heineman, 2012).

1991

The Diana municipal preschool in Reggio Emilia was named the world's most advanced early childhood education by the American weekly magazine Newsweek (Stremmel, 2012).

1994

Reggio Children, an international centre for the defence and promotion of children's rights and potentials, Reggio children as well as the International Association of Friends of Reggio Children was founded at the same time (Thornton & Brunton, 2014).

2003

A new initiative called the Istituzione of Preschools and Infant-toddler Centres to oversee educational services was launched by Robinson and Anna Frank (Reggio Emilia Approach, n.d.).

2006

The Loris Malaguzzi International Centre, a project formed from Loris Malaguzzi's vision to valorise creativity in children of all ages, was inaugurated.

2011

The Worldwide Affiliation of Companions of Reggio Children got to be the Fondazione Reggio Children - Centro Loris Malaguzzi (Rinaldi, 2021).

2012

The Loris Malaguzzi Universal Centre was completed, which provides a purposely social place for investigation and growth and the creation of a modern culture of children and humanity (Rinaldi, 2021).
